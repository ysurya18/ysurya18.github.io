  • Tale of Two Trajectories

    Visualization / Data Storytelling

    Dec 2024 - Apr 2025

    “A Tale of Two Trajectories” is a data storytelling project analyzing MISO survey responses from 2018, 2021, and 2024 to understand how students, faculty, and staff perceive and use academic digital services over time. Using Python based cleaning and analysis, I transformed nine raw survey files into standardized datasets, replaced inconsistent values, engineered new features, and built year over year comparison structures across importance, satisfaction, and usage metrics. Through time series analysis, gap analysis, and visualization (Tableau), our team uncovered a key insight: although services like Wi-Fi and the Learning Management System deliver high satisfaction, students increasingly undervalue them because they operate “so well they become invisible.” Meanwhile, faculty reliance on classroom technology continues to rise. This revealed a growing perception–performance disconnect that has strategic implications for digital service investment and communication. The final narrative combined analytics with storytelling to support university leadership in understanding evolving needs, validating post-pandemic investments, and planning student focused digital engagement strategies.
